Mkasanga Community Clinic

Location · South Luangwa, Zambia

Category · Healthcare

Status · In Motion

Mkasanga sits at the edge of South Luangwa — one of Africa's last great wilderness areas, and one of its most isolated communities. The nearest hospital is six to eight hours away on a good day. During the rains, the roads close entirely. Pregnant mothers travel alone. Children with fevers wait.

Already half-built by the people who need it.

The people of Mkasanga didn't wait for outside help. They started building their own clinic — by hand, with local materials, on the belief that their community deserved basic care. What they need now is for the rest of the world to notice.

House of Navi arrived with a spotlight, not a script. We're walking with Mkasanga for as long as it takes — documenting the build, amplifying the voices behind it, and connecting them to the donors and partners who believe what we believe: that stories come first, and funding follows.

5 months

Every year, Mkasanga becomes an island.

December through April, the Luangwa River rises. Bridges wash away. Roads submerge. The village is completely cut off — no emergency vehicle in, no one out.

Nestled on the eastern edge of South Luangwa National Park — a community of 3,500 building its own future, fuelled by generations of determination.

Geographic & Access Challenges

Getting to healthcare is not a matter of inconvenience. It is a life-or-death calculation made under pressure — in the dark, in the rain, with no vehicle and no bridge.

No Road, No Help

Even in the dry season, emergency access is near-impossible. There are no paved roads, no ambulance routes, and no infrastructure built for crisis response.

Distance & Terrain

In the dry season, Mfuwe Hospital is hours away over rough roads. Most families have no vehicle and cannot afford transport.

Under-Resourced on Arrival

Families who make it find a hospital stretched beyond capacity — limited staff, little equipment, and no guarantee of care.

Health Challenges & Vulnerabilities
Maternal & Child Risk

Women give birth without midwives or emergency obstetric care. Complications manageable in minutes at a clinic take lives in Mkasanga.

Endemic Disease

Malaria, cholera, tuberculosis, and HIV/AIDS are constant threats. Without testing and treatment nearby, preventable diseases turn fatal.

Untreated Conditions

Minor injuries and common infections escalate rapidly without nearby care — particularly during the rainy season when the village is cut off.

Human-Wildlife Conflict

On the edge of South Luangwa, snake bites and encounters with elephants, hippos, and crocodiles happen regularly. Emergency care does not exist.
